      Getting Started

      The practitioners of this art would cast their so-called Hexes by drawing strange patterns in the air with a Staff -- or craft powerful magical items to do the casting for them. How might I do the same?

      My intent with media

      I have long suspected that media and mind are not so separate as they seem. Only now do I begin to see how thought may be given form.

      By constraining media with intent, I can compel it to manifest in the world around me. These manifestations may be perceived, and more curiously, they may accept further intent, carrying thought forward until it becomes action.

      Contained Splinter

      Contained Splinter

      Planting a villager's mind into a box etched to channel the caster's will creates a Contained Splinter. It is made by flaying a novice villager into a block of slate.

      The Contained Splinter can hold a focus. Insert a focus carrying a hex, and when the circle reaches the block it manifests a splinter and waits for that splinter to finish before the circle proceeds.


      While active, the contained splinter casts as part of the circle and uses circle and splinter ambit rules. Using Renew Splinter with Locate Splinter's current position can create unusual self-looping behavior.

      A redstone signal works as an interrupt: it immediately dispels the active anchored splinter and allows the spell circle to continue.

      Intent

      An Intent is an imposition of will on the media, shaping it into a vessel awaiting a spark of interaction to awaken it.

      By themselves, intents are inert. A button waits for a press, an input for text, a numeric input for precise values, a slider for a nudge, a checkbox for a simple toggle, a selectable list for choosing iotas, and a dropdown for a selection. But once roused, they execute their patterns with the fervor of a cast hex.

                                                              Splinters

                                                              In trying to recreate Greater Sentinel, I first hypothosised that strong intent was the key. Instead I split away unstable slivers of my own will: brief-minded splinters that wait, cast once, and perish.

                                                              A splinter casts as me and draws on my media, yet its ambit is only sixteen blocks from its own position. If its cast mishaps, the splinter simply collapses rather than rebounding upon me.


                                                              Manifest Splinter (position vec, delay_ticks num, payload [iotas] → none)

                                                              • Renders
                                                                • Palettes
                                                                  Your browser does not support visualizing patterns. Pattern code: dedade

                                                                  Schedules a new splinter that will execute a stored payload after a delay. Player-cast splinters must begin within my own ambit and snap to the nearest half-block.

                                                                  Inputs:
                                                                  position: world-space vector to summon at.
                                                                  delay ticks: non-negative delay before execution.
                                                                  payload: non-empty list of iotas to store and execute.

                                                                  Cost: 5 dust times (active splinters + 1).


                                                                  Renew Splinter (position vec, delay_ticks num → none)

                                                                  • Renders
                                                                    • Palettes
                                                                      Your browser does not support visualizing patterns. Pattern code: dedaded

                                                                      Cast only by a splinter. Creates a successor splinter using the current splinter's stored payload and ravenmind.

                                                                      Inputs:
                                                                      position: world-space vector for the successor.
                                                                      delay ticks: non-negative delay before the successor executes.

                                                                      Cost: replacing at the same snapped position is free; moving to a new position costs 0.5 dust.

                                                                              Hex Trail (position vec, color_from vec, color_to vec, ticks num, id num, type? num → none)

                                                                              • Renders
                                                                                • Palettes
                                                                                  Your browser does not support visualizing patterns. Pattern code: qaqead

                                                                                  Creates a smooth, stateful trail channel keyed by id. Each cast appends one new point at position; the prior point is remembered automatically. Segments longer than 10 blocks do not connect.

                                                                                  Inputs:
                                                                                  position: world-space vector for the new point.
                                                                                  color from: RGB vector (0..1) at the start of the color cycle.
                                                                                  color to: RGB vector (0..1) at the end of the color cycle.
                                                                                  ticks: positive transition duration for color ping-pong.
                                                                                  id: numeric trail channel key; reuse to continue the same trail.
                                                                                  type?: optional style index, rounded and clamped to 0..14 (default 0).

                                                                                  Types: 0 dust ribbon, 1 sparks, 2 trail, 3 pulse beads, 4 end rod, 5 witch plume, 6 enchant flecks, 7 soul flame beads, 8 solid beam, 9 ring lattice, 10 lightning arc, 11 hex conjure ribbon, 12 hex conjure sparks, 13 hex conjure trail, 14 dual-helix.
                                                                                  Color vectors tint types 0-3 and 8-14; types 4-7 use native particle colors.
